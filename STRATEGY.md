# STRATEGY.md — Go-to-market

## The core problem
Replacing a lab's LIS is painful: data-integrity risk, retraining, downtime fear. A lab director won't swap a working system on a promise. So the LIS — our eventual flagship — cannot be the entry product.

## The wedge strategy
Enter with **light, low-switching-cost side tools** that:
1. deliver fast, visible value,
2. ideally create data/workflow dependencies that make our LIS the natural next step,
3. build trust so the eventual migration feels safe.

Sell sequence: side tool → read-only access to their data → free diagnostic → expansion modules → (only then) LIS.

## The hero motion: the free diagnostic ("diagnostic offert")
Instead of claiming a number, we **show** the lab its own number. We analyze ~3 months of their billing + analyzer data and hand them a real, attributable figure: denied claims, tests run but never billed, payment gaps. This doubles as the sales pitch AND the wedge. Proof > promise.

## On the "double revenue" claim
The user originally wanted "we double your annual revenue" as the headline. **We pushed back and the agreed position is:** do not headline it. It's not credible to a rational lab director and poisons the later LIS sale. The honest, persuasive version is "we show you the revenue you've already earned but never collected" — leakage to plug, not growth to invent.

Defensible benchmark figures (mostly US sources — treat as orders of magnitude, confirm via audit):
- 20–30% of a lab's billed revenue can go uncollected.
- Up to ~65% of denied claims are never reworked.
- Healthy net collection rate target ≈ 96%+.
- Moving from ~90% to ~96% net collection on a mid-size lab ≈ six figures/year.
- Analyzer downtime is the #1 cited pain (~73% of lab managers).
- Cutting pre-analytical TAT by ~30 min ≈ +20% urgent-care capacity (no new capex).

## Sales method (researched)
Challenger Sale + SPIN, adapted to a relational Algerian medical audience:
- **Teach** an insight they don't track ("do you know how much you actually *collect* vs bill?").
- **Ask** SPIN questions that surface the pain (net collection rate? who reworks denials?).
- **Prove** with their own data via the free diagnostic.
- Healthcare buying is multi-stakeholder, regulated, long-cycle, trust-driven → lead with trust, enter small.
- Different value story per role: a CFO pitch loses a medical director.

## Pricing framework (directional — validate locally)
- Wedge tools priced **low, near-impulse** for a lab director — you're buying the relationship and integration foothold; real margin comes later from the LIS + sticky modules.
- Free (loss leaders): the diagnostic/audit (it's the sales weapon) and the website (commodity; value is the hook + relationship).
- Never free: anything with real recurring cost (hosted LLM compute, heavy support).
- Models by product: middleware = per-analyzer or per-site; assistant = per-seat + usage; HR = per-employee; portal/dashboard = per-site or per-volume; RCM = % of recovered revenue OR flat; LIS = per-test-volume or per-site + implementation.

## LEGAL FLAG (repeated, important)
Percentage-of-recovered-revenue (RCM outcome pricing) on medical billing touches fee-splitting / anti-kickback rules that vary by country and can be restricted or banned. **Have an Algerian healthcare lawyer validate the structure before contracting or advertising it.**
