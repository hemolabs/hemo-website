# Conversion & trust rubric

Score each dimension per page. A page can be crystal-clear and still bounce — for this audience, **trust gates conversion**, so B/C/D usually decide the outcome. Convert the dimension reads into one 0–100 conversion-likelihood score per page (probability a real director submits the form): **bounce <35 / borderline 35–59 / convert ≥60**.

## A. Clarity & objective
- Value graspable in **≤5 seconds**; headline **≤8 words**, outcome-led not feature-led.
- A cold visitor understands *what's offered* AND *what to do next* in one read.
- One job per section; nothing competes with the primary message.

## B. Trust & credibility (usually the deciding block)
- **Reciprocity:** is a real human shown *before* asking for the prospect's number? Name + **Algerian phone to call themselves** + photo.
- **Identity:** raison sociale, ancrage/adresse en Algérie, mentions légales, hébergeur nommé. (54% of B2B buyers doubt a brand lacking clear company info; hiding contact reads as hiding something.)
- Trust signals sit **near the CTA**.
- Honest, specific claims over superlatives.

## C. Social proof (local)
- At least one **named lab + wilaya** (a confrère they can call). Specific names ≫ generic counts.
- "Premiers partenaires en cours d'installation" reads as **cobaye risk** — flag it; recommend a reserved, honest slot, never a fabricated testimonial.

## D. Funnel integrity & CTA
- **CTA promise == destination.** Any product button ("Voir / Essayer / Piloter / Recevoir") that opens an off-subject form is **bait-and-switch** → high-severity.
- CTA microcopy **first-person + outcome** ("Demander **mon** diagnostic", "**Voir ma** démo"). First-person lifts CTR materially.
- Reassurance microcopy around the CTA that pre-empts the next objection.

## E. Form UX
- **Fewest fields** (biggest single lever); **single column**; **top-aligned labels**; **mark required AND optional**; **inline (per-field) validation**.
- Low-commitment contact: **phone OR e-mail** (e-mail-only must be accepted for the risk-zero entry).
- Form copy on-subject; confidentiality stated at the field, not buried.

## F. Offer & economic model
- A **compelling, incentivised, zero/low-risk offer** (the diagnostic; the free site). Doctors act on a strong value prop + a reason to act now.
- Defuse "c'est gratuit, où est le piège ?": say **why** it's free, **what happens after**, and **"sans commission sur vos recettes"**. (Keep outcome/% pricing off the site pending legal sign-off.)
- No hidden-price anxiety: either a defensible range in DA or an explicit "no commission / no obligation" frame.

## G. Data & privacy (gating for billing + nominative data)
- Explain **how** data is accessed (export en lecture seule de SoftLAM/Winlabo; rien migré), **where hosted** (en Algérie), **who accesses**, **deletion**, **signed confidentiality** before access.
- Frame with **ISO 15189 / GBEA** on the page itself, not only a footer link. Transparency about security is itself a trust signal.

## H. Conformity honesty
- No "Conforme ISO 15189" (software isn't accredited). Use "conçu pour votre démarche…". An OAA auditor needs validable traceability, not "l'esprit de…".
- QC/traceability claims phrased as "historisés et exportables pour votre dossier d'accréditation".

## I. Healthcare UX & audience fit
- Calm, clean, low-anxiety aesthetic; ample whitespace; relational/human tone; "c'est vous qui décidez".
- Sequence: **relevance → credibility → process clarity → low-friction action**.
- Surface the **multi-site / billing-leak** pain for the highest-value prospects (it's their real concern).
- See `healthcare-ux.md` for detail.

## J. Accessibility for 45–60 readers
- Body **≥16px**, generous line spacing, **sans-serif** for body; contrast **≥4.5:1** (≥3:1 large text).
- Note: blues/purples fade for older eyes — don't rely on low-contrast blue for important text.
- Simple navigation, single-function menus; tap targets large.

## K. Honesty of numbers
- Label all mock UI (KPIs, monitors, chat, reservation) as **"· exemple"**.
- Source any stat or remove it; qualify estimates ("ordre de grandeur, à confirmer sur vos données"). Unsourced precision (e.g. a bare "73 %") reads as plaquette.

## Severity guide
- **Blocker** (caps page at bounce): B reciprocity missing, D bait-and-switch, C zero local proof, G data story absent on a data-touching page.
- **Major:** F unexplained "free", H conformity overpromise, K mock data unlabelled, I wrong pain for the target.
- **Polish:** A/E/J refinements once the blockers are gone.
