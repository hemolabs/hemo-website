# Research-backed principles (the evidence behind the rubric)

General CRO / UX / B2B-marketing findings, filtered to what applies to a skeptical, relationship-driven medical-B2B audience. Healthcare-specific UX is in `healthcare-ux.md`.

## Trust & credibility — the dominant lever here
- **54%** of B2B buyers doubt a brand that lacks clear company info; **52%** check the About page first. Hiding contact info reads as "something to hide".
- Trust signals near the CTA drive **~34–42%** conversion lifts; **naming specific clients** ("Dr X, Laboratoire Y, Sétif") beats generic counts ("des laboratoires partenaires").
- B2B buyers are skeptical because they spend company money and stake their reputation → honest, specific claims beat superlatives; original/first-party proof carries the most weight.
- Real photos > stock/illustration, especially for older/healthcare audiences: a real customer photo tested **~35% more sign-ups** than the best stock shot; staged stock can hurt trust *more than no image*. Use a real founder/team photo, never a fake-initials avatar.

## Hero & clarity
- **5-second test**: value must be graspable in ≤5s. Headlines **under ~8 words**, outcome over features. Users spend **~80%** of time above the fold.
- Clever/vague headlines lose; clarity wins. Save personality for the subhead.

## CTA microcopy
- **First-person** phrasing ("Demander **mon** diagnostic") lifts CTR materially vs "votre"; action + concrete outcome converts substantially better than generic "Envoyer".
- Microcopy around the button should pre-empt the next objection.

## Form UX (NN/g, Baymard)
- **Fewer fields** is the single most reliable predictor of completion (a 11→4 reduction has shown ~+120%). **Single-column** (~78% one-try vs ~42% multi-column). **Top-aligned labels** = faster, fewer errors. **Mark required AND optional**. **Inline validation** ~doubles first-attempt completion. Progressive profiling respects the user's time.

## Marketing to doctors / HCPs
- Doctors are time-poor and resist cold sales; win with **immediate credibility + genuinely valuable content + a compelling, incentivised offer**, and a reason to act now.
- Reach them by **e-mail** (their #1 business channel), not cold calls. The "diagnostic offert" is the compelling offer; e-mail capture matches their channel. Communicate intellectually and emotionally, like real people — never plaid-suit salesy.

## Design for credibility (2026 SaaS)
- "Trust-driven design" + **clarity-led minimalism**: every element must earn its place by moving the user toward the goal. Clean, fast, logically navigable sites signal an organized, dependable company; clutter/slowness erodes trust fast.
