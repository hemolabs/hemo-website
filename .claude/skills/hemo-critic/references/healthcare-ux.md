# Healthcare-specific UX & accessibility

Apply on top of the general rubric whenever the page touches clinical work, patient data, or the 45–60 reader.

## Trust-by-design (healthcare)
- **Calm, clean aesthetics reduce anxiety and build trust**: soft colours, ample whitespace, clear typography, strong visual hierarchy. Avoid clutter, dark/acid tech looks, and dense walls of text.
- Healthcare buyers/visitors decide in a sequence: **relevance first → credibility second → process clarity third → low-friction next action**. Pages that establish "this is for a lab like mine", then "these people are real/credible", then "here's exactly how it works", then a small next step, outperform.
- Transparency about **data security/handling is itself a trust signal** — patients and directors share sensitive info only when assured it's protected. State *where data is hosted, who accesses it, how it's deleted, and that a confidentiality agreement is signed before access*. For this market frame with **ISO 15189 / GBEA / OAA and hosting in Algeria** — never HIPAA/GDPR/CLIA.

## Clinical-software credibility (the demo cards)
- Show you know the workflow: real instrument models, real protocols, real QC language read as "ils connaissent le terrain". Keep them but **label mocks "· exemple"** so they aren't mistaken for fabricated proof.
- Respect the director's authority and accreditation: for anything automated (e.g. ordonnance reading), state **"l'outil pré-remplit, votre technicien valide, vous gardez le dernier mot"** and who is responsible. Error-prevention, consistency, and clear feedback are core clinical-UX principles.
- The interface/claim must serve the **clinical workflow**, not the reverse; surface the role-specific value (biologiste vs gestionnaire vs technicien) — a CFO/gestionnaire pitch loses a medical director and vice-versa.

## Accessibility for 45–60 readers (this is the core demographic)
- Body text **≥16px** with generous line spacing; offer comfortable sizing. Many older readers use reading glasses.
- **Sans-serif** for body (more legible at smaller sizes); reserve the serif display for headings only.
- Contrast **≥4.5:1** for body, **≥3:1** for large text (WCAG 2.1). **Blues and purples fade with age** — don't put important text in low-contrast blue on warm paper; verify the palette's blues meet contrast.
- **Simple navigation**, single-function menus, minimal sublevels; large tap targets; no reliance on hover.
- The EU now enforces accessibility legally (EAA, 2025); even outside the EU, accessible = more credible and reaches more of a 45–60+ audience.

## Quick healthcare checklist
- [ ] Calm/clean, low-anxiety layout; no clutter or hype.
- [ ] relevance → credibility → process → action sequence present.
- [ ] Data handling explained on-page (access, hosting in Algeria, deletion, confidentiality), framed ISO 15189/GBEA.
- [ ] Automation shows human-in-the-loop + responsibility.
- [ ] Mock UI labelled "· exemple".
- [ ] Body ≥16px sans-serif, contrast ≥4.5:1, blues not relied on for key text.
- [ ] Role-specific value surfaced (esp. billing/multi-site for gestionnaires).
